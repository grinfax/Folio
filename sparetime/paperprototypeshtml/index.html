Paper Prototyping

I've never been one to do things the normal way.

If I could use one adjective to describe myself it would be extraordinary. For example, taking notes in school.

It started when I stopped using traditional college rule notebooks, and started writing my notes on white printer paper. The college rule notebooks felt so restricting and quite old school if im being honest. Using printer paper allowed me to write my notes in various sizes and orientations, also I could drawing pictures and diagrams that aided in my understanding.

I found myself procrastinating less and studying more.

I thought this was a revelation but the revelation was just beginning to unfold.

I walked into OfficeMax one day getting last-minute school supplies and saw an assortment of colored printer paper on sale. Without hesitation I purchased pink, purple, orange, yellow, green, and red printer paper. That's when the game really changed.

With paper in abundance and a plethora of colors to choose from I started to get Crafty.

Being a to do list type person, I enjoy making lists of tasks to be completed. Even more so, I enjoy checking things off of my list's. I took the to do list concept and modified it to better fit my workflow.

I handled my tasks by priority, and I reshaped my tasks into projects; seeing that my tasks almost always required the completing of sub tasks. Breaking my projects into small sub tasks to be finished helped keep me on the track to completion.

Also, being able to cut off a completed sub task and literally "stick it" to its project's paper gave me a feeling of satisfaction and accomplishment.

Having the colored paper at my disposal added an extra layer of organization to my madness. There is a color scheme to each project that is not to be interrupted.

Each project has a separate but equally pleasing color scheme, unless a project has a direct correlation to another. In that case the projects will share the same color scheme

Not too long after this I started using my paper to make prototypes.

Paper prototyping gave me a new way to visualize user interface elements and conceptualize how said elements would interact with one another. Moving elements on my paper iPhone screen was as easy as peeling them off and re sticking them in different locations.

Paper prototyping allows me the fluidity to explore different possibilities of representing data in a very visual and concrete manner.

Currently, I paper prototype in the beginning stages of app development.

I use them as models to see how I want to display the data in my application and see if it can be replication in an integrated development environment(IDE).

I plan to continue to work on my prototypes, attempting to make them more complex and look more similar to a digital prototype.
